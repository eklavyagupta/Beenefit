Vorroa Check

1. Gear up with listed equipment:
Powdered sugar, ½ cup measuring cup marked at 100ml or 0.42 cups, Container with white bottom, sugar roll jar, and water.

2. Find a frame of brood and shake it hard once above the container.

3. Collect a 1/2 cup of nurse bees from the container and put them into the jar.

4. Add 2 tbsp powdered sugar in the jar.

5. Roll the jar gently to coat the bees with sugar.

6. Rest the jar aside in the shade for at least 2 minutes.(necessary)

7. Shake the mites out of the jar into a tub and return the bees from the jar back to the hive.

8. Dissolve the sugar with water so the mites are clear to count.

9. Count the mites and compare to the threshold of 3% infestation.(9 mites in the 1/2 cup sample)

Ant Check

1. Ants can usually be seen between the inner cover and the top cover. They also can be seen on the feeder if bees are being fed.

2. It would be a serious warning if the ant nest with eggs was seen in the hive.

Missing queen Check

There are several indicators for queen-missing hives:

1. A drop in bee population.

2. Brood and eggs are missing.

3. Queen cells appear.

4. Brood cells are taken for pollen and honey stores.

5. A large number of drones.

Robbed hive Check

There are several indicators for robbed hives:

1. The number of bees flying around the hive and especially the entrance seems larger than usual.

2. The bees flying around the hive seem agitated and/or more aggressive.

3. Bees trying to enter the hive through the bottom or the back of the hive.

4. Bees sway from side to side as they fly around the entrance and generally around the hive.

5. Increased commotion around the entrance into the hive.

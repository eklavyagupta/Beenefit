• Bee Activities · Queen egg production increases due to longer daytimes.
    · The population of bees grows as the brood nest growing.
    · Swarming might happen.
    Beekeeper Activities     · Recommended that 9-14 day interims for inspection.
    · Purchasing queen bees from queen breeders if needed.
    · Avoid swarming:
    1) Avoid congestion: provide more room before building the hive or add more honey super.
    2) Provide To ventilation: Drill holes in the honey supers and don’t cover the ventilation hole.
    Spring
    Sep - Nov
  • Bee Activities · Queen egg production peaks on December then starting to decrease.
    · The population of bees keeps growing while the brood nest remains in a large scale.
    · The chance of swarming drops as the queen cell decreasing.
    Beekeeper Activities     · Recommended that 3-6 week interim for inspection.
    · Best time to buy queen bee from queen breeders if you need.
    · follow the steps to avoid swarming above if needed.
    Summer
    Dec - Feb
  • Bee Activities · Queen egg production starts to drops in March and hit the bottom on May. · same as the number of brood nests and population.
    · Worker bees would force drones to leave the hive.
    Beekeeper Activities     · Do thorough inspection on March for bee health before winter comes.
    · Reduce the hive entrance to a width of 50 mm and remove surplus supers.
    · Leave enough honey in the hive to carry the colony through the winter.
    (at least 18 kg honey for 8-frame double hive)
    Autumn
    Mar - May
  • Bee Activities · Queen egg production, brood nests and population remains at minimum but slowly start to increase from July.

    Beekeeper Activities     · Renewal of bee registration.
    · Don't open the hive for checking of frames.
    · Inspect hives from the exterior, keep the entrance clear, no obstacles like grass.
    · Get ready for next season: prepare boxes, frames and equipment.
    Winter
    Jun - Aug
